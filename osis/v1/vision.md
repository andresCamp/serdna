# serdna.dev — Product Vision

## What This Is

serdna.dev is the personal portfolio site for Andres Campos. It exists to convert hiring decision makers — CTOs, engineering managers, recruiters at AI-forward companies — into meetings. The site is not a resume. It is a controlled demonstration of capability, judgment, and craft.

The site itself is the first case study.

## The Four Pillars

Everything on the site proves one or more of these. If a section doesn't map to a pillar, it doesn't belong.

### 1. First Principles Thinker — The Root

The root capability. Doesn't start from frameworks, trends, or domain expertise. Starts from: what's the actual problem, what does the user need, what's the simplest system that solves it.

This is innate — recognized before it became a trend. It's why he ships across wildly different domains, why he picks the right AI architecture instead of the popular one, and why his output is disproportionate to headcount.

### 2. Unreasonably Effective — The Result

Seven products. Seven domains. Two years. The work speaks for itself, but the case studies reveal why — each one shows the thinking process, not just the output.

### 3. Judgment Over Hype — The Edge

Knows when NOT to pull the trigger. Tried autonomous agents on MyStory, measured the latency, chose stateless LLM calls because the UX demanded it. The market is full of people reaching for agents for everything. The person who knows when an agent is wrong for the problem is rarer and more valuable.

"Knowing when not to pull the trigger is just as important as when to pull the trigger."

### 4. Team Multiplier — The Hire-Me Signal

Led a 5-person dev team. Managed 20+ contributors including a 15-intern program. Taught a 12-week bootcamp and designed the curriculum. Runs a discovery-first client process — listens before building. Not a lone wolf. Someone who raises the ceiling for the people around them. First principles thinking is transferable — it makes everyone sharper.

## Guardrails

### Do

- Lead with work, not credentials.
- Frame founder background as range and depth, not flight risk.
- Position dev tools as team-leverage.
- Let case studies reveal the thinking process.
- Keep copy direct, specific, confident.
- The site itself must be evidence of craft — it is the first case study.

### Don't

- Say "nontraditional path" — show it.
- Mention wanting to be a founder, building on the side, or bridge jobs.
- Over-explain — let the work speak.
- Sound philosophical or visionary — wrong register.
- List every technology — show judgment about which tool for which problem.
- Chase trends in the design — the site should feel considered, not fashionable.
