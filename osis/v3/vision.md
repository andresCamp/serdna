# serdna.dev v3 — Vision

## What Changed

v2 overcomplicated the pitch. Seven sections, hover quadrants, systems grids, language breakdowns — all trying to *prove* capability instead of *showing* it. The work is the proof. Everything else is noise.

v3 strips to the essential: hero, work, contact. A visitor scans the page, sees the output, and either reaches out or doesn't. No fluff, no theater.

---

## Core Belief

**The work speaks. Everything else gets out of the way.**

---

## The Reaction Test

A founder or CTO lands on serdna.dev. Within 10 seconds they see: name, title, four shipped products with clear context for each. They think: "This person builds real things. I want to talk to them."

The page is scannable. Every project earns its spot by proving something specific. If they want depth, they click. If they don't, the surface already did its job.

---

## What Stays from v2

- **Positioning:** 0-to-1 builder with taste and judgment. Product engineer, not just a coder.
- **Dual-track strategy:** Freelance now, full-time remote as bridge.
- **The four pillars:** Builder with Taste, Unreasonably Effective, Judgment Over Hype, Team Multiplier. They're just proven through the work now, not through dedicated sections.
- **Hero:** Parallax portrait, name, title, CTAs. The craft proof lives here.
- **The site as case study:** Still true. The implementation quality of the page itself is evidence.

## What Dies

- Digital rain (preserved in `/components` showcase)
- Bento grid (replaced by alternating featured work)
- How I Build quadrants section
- Systems/tech grid section
- Language breakdown
- Values section
- Timeline section

## What's New

- **Alternating featured work** — 4 projects, each proving one thing, scannable in seconds
- **Carousel** — 3 more projects with CTA to full feed
- **`/projects` feed** — all projects, browsable
- **`/components` page** — digital rain and other showcase pieces
- **Modal deep dives** — click "read more" for case study content (later pass)

---

## Guardrails

### Do
- Lead with work, not words
- Make every project card earn its spot with a specific proof point
- Keep the page scannable — a visitor should "get it" without scrolling twice
- Maintain craft quality in implementation — the site is still the first case study
- Show tech stack per project so evaluators can instantly qualify

### Don't
- Add sections that explain rather than show
- Over-design the cards — let the project images and copy do the work
- Build depth before the surface is right
- Add features that don't serve the "scan → signal → reach out" flow
